The Challenge

Problem → Solution

The Problem

Most small and mid-sized stores are still running on a mix of paper notebooks, spreadsheets, and POS machines nobody fully trusts. Spreadsheets don't stop two staff editing the same stock count. Paper gets rewritten after the fact. And most POS software assumes a connection you can't always guarantee.

The Solution

RetailOS installs on a PC inside your store and becomes the single place every sale, price, and stock count lives. Staff phones on the shop's own WiFi connect straight to it — recording sales at the counter, updating stock in real time, feeding into reports automatically. The system runs locally first: your shop's day-to-day operation doesn't depend on an internet connection.

What's Built Today

Features

Only real, implemented features. No roadmap items here.

Sales recording with real-time stock deduction

Multi-item sales, backend-verified pricing, stock checked before every sale commits — insufficient stock rejects the whole transaction, never a partial one.

20-minute edit window

Staff can correct a sale shortly after completing it; managers and admins can edit any sale, with every change logged.

Inventory management

Add, edit, and soft-delete products — historical sales always keep their original product reference, even after a product is discontinued.

Purchase & restock approval flow

Any role can log a restock; stock only updates once an admin approves it, with every movement recorded.

Live reporting

Daily, monthly, and yearly sales and profit reports, top-selling items, and per-employee performance — computed directly from the database, not a cached snapshot.

Role-based access

Admin, manager, and employee permissions enforced at the API level, not just hidden in the UI.

Full audit trail

Every sale, edit, cancellation, product change, and login is logged and immutable.

Device-aware authentication

One active login per user — logging in elsewhere signs out the old session automatically.

Local-First Architecture

How It Works

The store PC is the server

RetailOS runs on the computer already behind the counter — this PC is the single source of truth for everything sold, priced, and stocked.

Staff phones connect over WiFi

No mobile data, no external internet connection needed to record a sale or check stock — just a browser and the local network.

Reports update automatically

No manual tallying at closing — every transaction already feeds into the day's numbers.

Current Scope

RetailOS today is a single-PC, real-time system — what you see reflects what's actually in the database at that moment. Offline queuing (continuing to sell if the shop WiFi itself goes down, then syncing later) is on our roadmap and not yet part of the current build. We built the core transaction and inventory engine first, because that's what a store needs to trust before anything else.

Architecture

Technical Advantage

No cloud dependency for daily operation

Your data lives on hardware you control, in your store.

Backend-verified everything

Prices, stock levels, and totals are always recalculated server-side — never trusted from the client.

Immutable audit logging

Every sensitive action is permanently recorded, supporting accountability without micromanagement.

All-or-nothing sales

No partial commits — a sale either fully succeeds or is fully rejected, protecting stock accuracy.

Built to extend

The architecture is structured so multi-store and offline-sync capability can be layered on without a rebuild.
